About Austen Aloud

Hilary Havens, Gerard Cohen-Vrignaud, and Terry Weymouth

The project

Austen Aloud presents Jane Austen's six novels with every passage of dialogue attributed to its speaker, built from the TEI encodings of the Austen Said project. It offers speech statistics for each novel, a chapter-by-chapter reading interface with a play-script view for classroom read-alouds, and a searchable concordance of dialogue and narration across all six novels.

The Language Lab's speaker demographics (gender, class or rank, marital status, age category) are the Austen Said editors' categories, reproduced verbatim from the TEI personography; characters the editors did not classify appear as “unrecorded.” Letters are the passages the TEI marks as embedded correspondence. Vocabulary density is unique words divided by total words (type–token ratio) — a rough measure of how varied a speaker's vocabulary is.

Download the data

austen.sqlite — a SQLite database of all six novels: book, speaker, speech_act (every attributed passage with chapter, conversation, and speech-act indices), conversation_word (every spoken word), and book_stats (per-speaker aloud / not-aloud word and character counts). The speaker table carries the Austen Said demographic fields (sex, soc_class, marital, age_cat, trait), and speech_act / conversation_word flag letter text with in_letter.
